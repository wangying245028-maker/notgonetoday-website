Privacy of the Living

Last Updated: January 2026

1. Introduction

Welcome to Not Gone Today! We handle your data with one goal in mind: making sure someone knows if you've stopped checking in. This policy explains exactly what we collect and how we use it to keep you connected to the world of the living.

2. What We Collect

To implement friend sharing and emergency notification features, we collect the following information:

  • Device ID: Used as your unique identifier to manage your status and connections without requiring personal account information.
  • Check-in Data: We store your latest check-in time and latest check-in photo. These are used to show your friends you're safe. We only keep the most recent one—older records are overwritten.
  • Emergency Contact Details: The name and email of your Chosen One, so we know who to mail when you go quiet.
  • Safety Settings: Your timeout duration and the custom message you want us to send to your emergency contact.

This data is used exclusively for friend information sharing and automated emergency emails.

3. How Your Data is Handled

  • Server-Side Upload: The information listed above is uploaded to our server to facilitate real-time status updates for your retrieval squad and to trigger automated alerts.
  • No Monetization: We DO NOT sell, share, or monetize your data with third parties or advertisers.
  • No Invasive Tracking: We DO NOT track your real-time GPS location or raid your contact list.

4. Data Storage & Retention

4.1 Overwrite Policy

We believe in minimal storage. Your check-in history is not archived; each new check-in (time and photo) overwrites the previous one on our server.

4.2 Auto-Deletion Policy

Freshness Guarantee: If you fail to check in for 20 consecutive days, our system will automatically delete all of your server-side data. If you've truly gone AFK from life, your digital footprint with us disappears.
